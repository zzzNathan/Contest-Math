\begin{problem}{Irish Math Olympiad 1992 --- P6/10}
Let $n > 2$ be an integer and let $m = \sum k^3$, where the sum is taken
over all integers $k$ with $1 \leq k < n$ that are relatively prime to $n$.
Prove that $n$ divides $m$. (Note that two integers are
\textit{relatively prime} if, and only if, their greatest common divisor
equals 1.)
\end{problem}

\begin{solution}{Solution}
Let $q$ be a positive integer that is coprime with $n$.
Due to Euclid's algorithm $n-q$ is also coprime with $n$. Thus if we
consider the sum modulo $n$, when $k = q$ we know that $q^3$ is contributed
to the sum however since $k = n-q$ will also appear we know that $-q^3$
will also be contributed to the sum. In total these values of $k$ have
contributed $0$ to the sum mod $n$.\\

Since we can pair up all of the integers $k$ that are coprime with $n$ like
so $(q, n-q)$ and we know that these values of $k$ will contribute 0 to
the sum mod $n$, we conclude that $m \equiv 0 \ \text{(mod } n)$,
that is $n \mid m$. $\blacksquare$
\end{solution}
