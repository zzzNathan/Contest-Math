\begin{problem}{Irish Math Olymipad 1989 --- P9/10}
Let $a$ be a positive real number, and let

$$
b = \sqrt[3]{a + \sqrt{a^2 + 1}} + \sqrt[3]{a - \sqrt{a^2 + 1}}.
$$

Prove that $b$ is a positive integer, if and only if, $a$ is a positive
integer of the form $\frac12 n(n^2 + 3)$.
\end{problem}

\begin{solution}{Solution}
Let $x = \sqrt[3]{a + \sqrt{a^2 + 1}}$ and
$y = \sqrt[3]{a - \sqrt{a^2 + 1}}$. Notice that
$xy = \sqrt[3]{a^2 - (a^2 + 1)} = -1$. \\

Observe the following,
\[
\begin{aligned}
b^3 &= \left(a + \sqrt{a^2 + 1}\right) + 3xy(x+y) + \left(a - \sqrt{a^2 + 1}\right) \\
&= 2a + 3(-1)b \\
\end{aligned}
\]

and so $b^3 + 3b = 2a$ giving $a = \frac12 b(b^2 + 3)$ for some
positive integer $b$.
\\

\textit{Remark: This is a pretty dumb problem in that the most braindead
approach (just cube both sides) actually works. It looks intimidating but
is actually just really easy.}
\end{solution}
