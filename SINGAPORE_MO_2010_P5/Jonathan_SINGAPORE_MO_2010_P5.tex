\begin{problem}{Singapore Math Olympiad 2010 --- P5/5}
Given $a_1\ge 1$ and $a_{k+1}\ge a_k+1$ for all $k = 1,2,\dots,n$, show that $$a_1^3+a_2^3+\dots+a_n^3\ge (a_1+a_2+\dots+a_n)^2.$$
\end{problem}

\begin{solution}{Solution}
Notice that $a_{k+1} \geq a_k + 1 \geq (a_{k-1} + 1) + 1 \geq \cdots \geq
a_1 + k \geq 1 + k$, so we can conclude that $a_k \geq k$. Recall the
formulae for sum of the first $n$ positive integer cubes, and for sum of
the first $n$ positive integers.

$$
a_1^3 + a_2^3 + \cdots + a_n^3 \geq 1^3 + 2^3 + \cdots + n^3 \geq
\left[ \frac{n(n + 1)}{2} \right]^2
$$
and
$$
a_1 + a_2 + \cdots + a_n \geq 1 + 2 + \cdots + n \geq \frac{n(n + 1)}{2}
$$

Thus

$$
\frac{a_1^3 + a_2^3 + \cdots + a_n^3}{(a_1 + a_2 + \cdots + a_n)^2}
\geq 1
$$

which reduces to the desired inequality upon multiplying both sides by
$(a_1 + a_2 + \cdots + a_n)^2$. $\blacksquare$
\end{solution}
