\begin{problem}{Harvard-MIT Math Tournament February Guts 2017 --- P9}
Jeffrey writes the numbers 1 and $10^8$ on
the blackboard. Every minute, if $x$, $y$ are on the board, Jeffery
replaces them with
$$
\frac{x+y}{2} \quad \text{and} \quad
2\left( \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} \right)^{-1}
$$

After 2017 minutes the two numbers are $a$ and $b$. Find $\min(a, b)$ to
the nearest integer.
\end{problem}

\begin{solution}{Solution}
Observe that
$$
\frac{x+y}{2}\cdot 2\left( \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} \right)^{-1}
=
\frac{x+y}{2}\cdot \frac{2xy}{x+y} = xy
$$

and so under the given operation the product of the two numbers on the
board in invariant. Suppose that $y\geq x$. Then

\[
\begin{aligned}
\frac{x+y}{2} - \frac{2xy}{x+y} &< \frac{y-x}{2} \\
\frac{(y-x)^2}{2(x+y)} &< \frac{y-x}{2} \\
\frac{y-x}{x+y} &< 1 \\
\end{aligned}
\]

but this is true since $y-x < x+y$. Thus the gap between the two numbers
on the board reduces by more than half each minute. Formally let
$D_k$ be the non-negative difference between the two numbers on the
board after the $k$-th minute. Now since
$\log_2(10^8) = 8\log_2(10) < 32$ notice that

$$
D_{2017} < \frac{1}{2^{2017}}\cdot 10^8 < \frac{1}{2^{2017}}\cdot 2^{32}.
$$

So for some $\epsilon < \frac{1}{2^{1985}}$ the last two numbers on the
board are of the form $(k-\epsilon, k+\epsilon)$ and have product $10^8$.
Clearly they are both just very close to $\sqrt{10^8} = 10^4$ and so
the answer is $10^4$.
\end{solution}
