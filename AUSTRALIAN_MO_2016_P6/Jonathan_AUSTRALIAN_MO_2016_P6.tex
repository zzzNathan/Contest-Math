\begin{problem}{Australian Math Olympiad 2016 --- P5}
If $a,b,c$ are positive integers such that $a^3 + b^3 = 2^c$, prove that
$a = b$.
\end{problem}

\begin{solution}{Solution}
WLOG assume that $a \geq b$.
Let $g = \gcd(a,b)$. For some coprime positive integers $x,y$ we may write
$a = gx$ and $b = gy$. We have
$$
g^3(x^3 + y^3) = 2^c.
$$
Since $(x^3 + y^3) \mid 2^c$, this quantity must be a pure power of 2.
Since $x,y \in \mathbb{N}$ we have $x^3 + y^3 = 2^n$ for some
$n \in \mathbb{N}$. Reducing mod 2 we find that $x,y$ must have the same
parity, but they cannot both be even since $\gcd(x,y) = 1$, so the only
possiblity is that $x,y$ are both odd. Notice that
$$
x^3 + y^3 = (x + y)(x^2 - xy + y^2) = 2^n.
$$
Since both $x,y$ are odd we observe that
$x^2 - xy + y^2 \equiv 1 \ (\text{mod } 2)$. Since $x^3 + y^3$ is a pure
power of 2, the only possibility is that
$
x^2 - xy + y^2 = 1.
$
Now suppose FTSOC that $a > b$ or equivalently $x > y$. Then
$$
1 = x^2 - xy + y^2 > (y+1)^2 - (y+1)y + y^2 = y^2 + y + 1 \geq 3
$$
which is a contradiction. Thus $a\leq b$, but since we assumed also
$a\geq b$, the only possibility is $a = b$. $\blacksquare$
\end{solution}
